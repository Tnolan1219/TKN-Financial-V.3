3Ô∏è‚É£ Important insurance basics (easy words)

  • Put in 5% now: TSP is like a 401(k). Put at least 5% of your pay so you will catch the DoD match later. (DoD gives 1% automatically after ~60 days, then may match more after 2 years.)
  • Roth vs Traditional: Roth means you pay taxes now and take tax-free money later ‚Äî often good when you are early in your career. Traditional means you pay taxes later.
  • Simple fund choice: L‚ÄëFund is a one-click mix that gets safer as you near retirement. Good default.

5Ô∏è‚É£ Set up good bank and credit habits

  • Choose a main bank: Examples: USAA or Navy Federal. Look for low fees and a good mobile app.
  • One credit card to build credit: No annual fee, pay the full balance each month, keep usage under 10% of the limit.
  • Protect your credit: You can freeze or monitor your credit if worried about identity theft.

6Ô∏è‚É£ Career Starter Loan ‚Äî when it makes sense

  • What it is: A small personal loan (banks like Navy Federal or USAA offer them) to help with start‚Äëup costs (uniforms, car for work, moving).
  • Use it for needs, not wants: Good for things you must have to do your job; avoid using it for luxury items.
  • Plan to pay it off: Model monthly payments so they fit your budget; avoid long terms that cost more interest.

7Ô∏è‚É£ Renting now vs buying with the VA loan (plain talk)

  • VA loan simple: A home loan for eligible veterans/service members that often requires no down payment and does not need private mortgage insurance (PMI). There is an upfront "funding fee" you may finance into the loan (sometimes waived for certain disabilities).
  • When to rent: If your job or location might change soon, or you don‚Äôt have 3‚Äì6 months of savings, rent until things are stable.
  • House‚Äëhack idea: Buy a property with up to 4 units, live in one and rent the others ‚Äî but run numbers conservatively and keep money set aside for repairs.

8Ô∏è‚É£ Know simple legal protections

  • SCRA (simple): If you had loans before you joined, SCRA can lower some interest rates to 6% while you are on active duty. Send your orders to the lender and ask them to apply it in writing.
  • Leases & contracts: If you get orders to move, some leases or phone contracts can be ended early ‚Äî keep your orders and ask base legal if unsure.
  • Use base legal help: Free legal aid on base can help with landlord or contract questions.

Quick rule: Try to save about 20% of your base pay + BAS/BAH if possible. If not, increase contributions a little each pay period.

TSP (retirement savings) ‚Äî plain guide

  • What is TSP? A retirement account similar to a 401(k). You choose how much of your pay to save.
  • Roth vs Traditional: Roth = pay taxes now, money comes out tax-free later (good when taxes are low). Traditional = tax now later.
  • Simple pick: L‚ÄëFund is an easy default mix that gets more conservative as you near retirement.
  • Don't max out too early: Spreading contributions through the year helps make sure you get employer match every month.
Minimum to get match: Set at least 5% now so you won't miss out when DoD starts matching your contributions.

  • Red flags: luxury purchases, investing borrowed funds without risk plan, long loan term that outlasts the car.
  • Decision checks: Monthly payment ‚â§10% of take‚Äëhome; cash reserve ‚â•1 month after taking the loan; insurance affordable.
Pro move: If refinancing pre‚Äëservice debt under SCRA (6% cap), compare the total interest saved before consolidating.

Common money mistakes ‚Äî how to fix them

  • Ignore LES errors: If money is wrong, tell finance now. Small errors add up.
  • No emergency fund: Start with $1,000. Set up automatic monthly transfers into savings.
  • Missing TSP match later: Keep contributing at least 5% so you don‚Äôt miss free DoD money later.
  • Over‚Äëcar: Buying a car you can‚Äôt afford breaks the budget. Buy reliable with affordable payments.

Mini Glossary

LES ‚Äî Leave and Earnings Statement: your military pay stub that shows pay, allowances, and deductions.
BAH ‚Äî Basic Allowance for Housing: money to help pay for housing (rent or mortgage).
BAS ‚Äî Basic Allowance for Subsistence: money to help pay for food/meals.
TSP ‚Äî Thrift Savings Plan: your retirement account (like a 401(k)).
BRS ‚Äî Blended Retirement System: the military retirement system that includes automatic and matching TSP money.
